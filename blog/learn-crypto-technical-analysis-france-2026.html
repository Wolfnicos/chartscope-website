Why Learn Crypto Technical Analysis in France?

The French Crypto Landscape in 2026

France has become one of Europe’s most regulated crypto markets. With the AMF’s PSAN registration system and clear tax guidelines from the Direction Générale des Finances Publiques, more French residents are entering the space than ever before. But regulation doesn’t teach you how to read a chart.

The problem is simple: most French beginners open an exchange account, buy their first Bitcoin or Ethereum, and then stare at a confusing mass of red and green bars, squiggly lines, and strange acronyms like “RSI” and “MACD.” They have no idea what the chart is telling them.

The Cost of Not Understanding Charts

A beginner who doesn’t understand technical analysis might sell at the first red candle out of fear — or buy at the top because they see green and assume it will continue. Both mistakes are avoidable with basic education. Users who take even a few hours to learn candlestick patterns and momentum indicators typically make fewer impulsive decisions. They understand why a chart is moving, not just that it moved.

Key Technical Analysis Concepts Explained by Chartscope

Candlestick Patterns: The Language of Price Action

Every candlestick tells a story. The body shows the opening and closing price; the wicks show the high and low. But patterns form when multiple candles appear in sequence.

Here’s a step-by-step guide to identifying a bullish engulfing pattern using Chartscope’s AI Vision:

  1. Open Chartscope and navigate to the coin chart you want to analyze
  2. Take a screenshot of the chart (or use the in-app capture)
  3. Open AI Vision and upload the screenshot
  4. Read the explanation: Chartscope’s AI will tell you if it sees a bullish engulfing pattern, what it typically means, and what other indicators confirm or contradict it
  5. Ask follow-up questions in AI Chat: “Is this pattern reliable on the 4-hour timeframe?” or “What volume should I look for to confirm this?”

This workflow teaches pattern recognition through real examples, not abstract theory.

RSI & MACD: Momentum and Trend

Chartscope explains every technical indicator in educational context. For RSI (Relative Strength Index), the app will tell you: - What the current RSI value means (e.g., “RSI at 72 suggests the asset may be overbought”) - Why it matters (e.g., “overbought conditions sometimes precede reversals, but not always”) - What other indicators say (e.g., “MACD is still bullish, creating a conflict — here’s how to interpret that”)

For MACD, you learn to read the crossover signals and histogram momentum — again, without trading signals. Just clear, jargon-free explanations.
