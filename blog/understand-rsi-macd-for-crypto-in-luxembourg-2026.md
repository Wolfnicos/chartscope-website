### RSI Explained Simply

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) is a momentum oscillator that measures the speed and magnitude of recent price changes. It moves between 0 and 100, helping you understand whether an asset has moved too far in one direction too quickly.

| RSI Value Range | What It Suggests | Common Interpretation |
|-----------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| 70–100 | Overbought territory | Price may have risen too fast |
| 30–70 | Neutral zone | Normal price movement |
| 0–30 | Oversold territory | Price may have fallen too fast |

The RSI was developed by J. Welles Wilder in 1978 and remains one of the most widely used **crypto technical indicators** today. It doesn't predict where price will go next—it simply tells you when an asset is statistically stretched relative to its recent history.

### MACD Explained Simply

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) tracks the relationship between two moving averages of an asset's price. It consists of three components:

- **MACD line**: The difference between a 12-period and 26-period exponential moving average
- **Signal line**: A 9-period exponential moving average of the MACD line
- **Histogram**: The visual difference between the MACD line and the Signal line

When the MACD line crosses above the Signal line, it suggests upward momentum. When it crosses below, it suggests downward momentum. The histogram shows whether that momentum is accelerating or slowing down.

## FAQ

### What is the RSI indicator and how does it apply to crypto charts?

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) is a momentum oscillator that measures the speed and magnitude of recent price changes on a scale of 0 to 100. In crypto chart analysis, it helps beginners understand when an asset has moved significantly in one direction. Readings above 70 suggest overbought conditions, while readings below 30 suggest oversold conditions. RSI does not predict future price movements—it provides context about recent momentum extremes.

### How does the MACD indicator help in understanding crypto market momentum?

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) shows the relationship between two moving averages of price. It consists of the MACD line, Signal line, and histogram. When the MACD line crosses above the Signal line, it suggests increasing upward momentum. When it crosses below, it suggests increasing downward momentum. The histogram shows whether momentum is accelerating or slowing down. MACD helps beginners identify trend direction and strength without needing to calculate moving averages manually.
