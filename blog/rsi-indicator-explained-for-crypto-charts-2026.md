## What is the RSI Indicator and Why is it Important for Crypto?

### Understanding the Core Metric

The **Relative Strength Index crypto** indicator, developed by J. Welles Wilder in 1978, measures the speed and magnitude of recent price changes. It oscillates between 0 and 100, with readings above 70 traditionally considered "overbought" and readings below 30 considered "oversold." But here's where most explanations stop—and where the confusion begins.

**Why RSI matters specifically for cryptocurrency:**

Cryptocurrency markets are 24/7, highly volatile, and driven by sentiment as much as fundamentals. Unlike traditional stock markets with circuit breakers and trading hours, crypto can swing 20% in a single hour. RSI helps you quantify whether that swing is losing steam or gaining momentum.

| RSI Reading | Traditional Label | What It Actually Means in Crypto |
|-------------|-------------------|----------------------------------|
| 70–100 | Overbought | Price moved up rapidly; trend may be strong or exhausted |
| 50–70 | Bullish momentum | Uptrend is active; buyers are in control |
| 30–50 | Bearish momentum | Downtrend is active; sellers are in control |
| 0–30 | Oversold | Price moved down rapidly; trend may be weak or reversing |

### Why Beginners Get It Wrong

The most common mistake? Treating RSI as a binary signal. A **crypto indicator explanation** that says "sell when RSI hits 70" ignores context. In a strong bull market, Bitcoin's RSI can stay above 70 for days while price continues climbing. Selling early means missing gains. Conversely, in a crash, RSI can stay below 30 longer than you can stay solvent.

**The real question RSI answers:** "Is the current price move accelerating or decelerating?" Not "Should I buy or sell?"

### Divergence Detection Made Simple

RSI divergence is one of the most powerful concepts in technical analysis, yet most beginners never learn it because it's hard to spot. **RSI chart analysis** for divergence means comparing price action with RSI direction:

- **Bullish divergence:** Price makes a lower low, but RSI makes a higher low → upward momentum building
- **Bearish divergence:** Price makes a higher high, but RSI makes a lower high → downward momentum building

Chartscope's AI automatically flags these divergences and explains them. The Enriched Insights feature includes a "Momentum" card that quantifies whether the current trend is accelerating or weakening.

### Practical Checklist for RSI Analysis

Use this checklist every time you analyze an RSI reading:

- [ ] **Check the trend:** Is the overall trend up, down, or sideways? (Use moving averages)
- [ ] **Look for divergence:** Is RSI moving opposite to price?
- [ ] **Verify with volume:** Is the RSI move supported by increasing volume?
- [ ] **Check indicator conflict:** Does MACD agree with RSI?
- [ ] **Consider volatility:** Is this coin naturally more volatile? (RSI extremes are more common in volatile coins)
- [ ] **Set context:** What's happening in the broader market? (Bitcoin dominance, news events)
