Swiss Tax Regulations for Cryptocurrency Investments

Switzerland has established clear regulations concerning cryptocurrency investments, classifying them as assets subject to wealth tax. According to the Swiss Federal Tax Administration (FTA), the following points are crucial for investors:

  1. Wealth Tax: Cryptocurrency holdings are taxed as part of an individual’s total wealth. The rates vary depending on the canton.
  2. Income Tax: If cryptocurrencies are traded for profit, investors may be liable for income tax. This is particularly applicable for professional traders.
  3. Capital Gains Tax: Private investors are often exempt from capital gains tax on cryptocurrencies, provided trading is not conducted as a business activity.

These regulations highlight the importance of keeping meticulous records of all transactions and valuations for accurate tax reporting.
