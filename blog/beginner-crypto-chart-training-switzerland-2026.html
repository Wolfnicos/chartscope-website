Understanding Crypto Charts: A Foundation for Beginners

Before you can analyze, you must understand the language of the chart. The two most common chart types are line charts and candlestick charts. For beginners, mastering candlesticks is the single most important step.

Candlestick Patterns: The Alphabet of Trading

A single candlestick tells you four things: the opening price, closing price, highest price, and lowest price within a specific time frame. When these candles form patterns, they tell a story.

Pattern Name What It Looks Like What It Suggests
Doji A cross or plus sign where open and close are nearly equal Market indecision; a potential trend reversal or pause
Bullish Engulfing A large green candle completely covering the previous red candle Strong buying pressure; a potential upward reversal
Shooting Star A small body at the bottom with a long upper wick Price pushed up but was rejected; a potential bearish reversal
Hammer A small body at the top with a long lower wick Price dropped but was bought back up; a potential bullish reversal

Learning to spot these formations is the foundation of chart patterns crypto education. Chartscope’s AI Vision feature can identify these patterns for you automatically from a screenshot, confirming what you see and providing a plain-language explanation.
