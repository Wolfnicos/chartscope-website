### RSI: The Relative Strength Index Explained Simply

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) is a momentum oscillator that measures the speed and magnitude of recent price changes. It ranges from 0 to 100 and helps you understand whether a cryptocurrency is potentially overbought or oversold.

- **RSI above 70**: Often considered overbought—prices may have risen too fast
- **RSI below 30**: Often considered oversold—prices may have fallen too far
- **RSI around 50**: Neutral territory, no strong directional bias

RSI was developed by J. Welles Wilder Jr. and remains one of the most widely used technical indicators in financial markets. For crypto traders in Switzerland, RSI helps answer a simple question: "Is this move sustainable, or is it getting extreme?"

### MACD: Moving Average Convergence Divergence

The MACD indicator shows the relationship between two moving averages of a cryptocurrency's price. It consists of three components:

| Component | What It Shows |
|-----------|---------------|
| MACD Line | Difference between 12-period and 26-period exponential moving averages |
| Signal Line | 9-period EMA of the MACD line |
| Histogram | Visual difference between MACD line and signal line |

When the MACD line crosses above the signal line, it suggests bullish momentum. When it crosses below, bearish momentum may be building. However, these signals are **educational observations**, not buy/sell commands.

## FAQ

### What is the RSI indicator and how does it apply to crypto charts?

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) is a momentum oscillator that measures price change speed and magnitude on a scale of 0-100. In crypto charts, RSI helps identify when an asset may be overbought (above 70) or oversold (below 30). However, in strong trends, RSI can remain in extreme zones for extended periods, so it should be used as one piece of context, not a standalone signal.

### How does the MACD indicator help in understanding cryptocurrency price movements?

The MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) shows the relationship between two exponential moving averages. When the MACD line crosses above the signal line, it suggests increasing bullish momentum. Crosses below indicate bearish momentum. The histogram visualizes this relationship's strength. MACD is most useful for identifying trend changes and momentum shifts, not predicting exact price targets.
