How Chartscope Simplifies Complex Indicators Like RSI & MACD

RSI: No Math Required

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) measures whether an asset is overbought or oversold. Traditional explanations throw formulas at you. Chartscope shows you a visual slider: when the RSI line crosses above 70, the asset is “hot” (overbought). When it dips below 30, it’s “cold” (oversold). That’s it. The AI then walks you through three real-world examples so you see the pattern in action.

For a deeper dive, read our dedicated guide on RSI and MACD indicators explained for crypto charts.

MACD: The Trend Detective

The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) indicator helps traders spot trend changes. Chartscope breaks it down into three simple components: - The MACD line (fast-moving average minus slow-moving average) - The signal line (a smoothed version of the MACD line) - The histogram (bars showing the difference between the two)

Instead of a wall of text, Chartscope uses color-coded arrows and real-time chart overlays to show you exactly when a buy or sell signal appears.

Pattern Recognition Made Visual

Chartscope also teaches candlestick patterns — doji, hammer, engulfing — by highlighting them directly on live charts. The AI draws circles around each pattern and explains what it means in the current market context. This visual approach is why many users call it the best AI crypto analysis tool for learning.
