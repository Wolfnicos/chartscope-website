Chartscope Review: AI Crypto Chart Analysis App 2026

A first-time crypto buyer opened Binance, saw a maze of red and green candles, and closed the app within 30 seconds. That moment of confusion is exactly why Chartscope exists as a crypto education app that doesn’t overwhelm beginners with jargon or trading signals. Instead, it uses on-device AI to explain what each candle, indicator, and pattern actually means — in plain language.

What is Chartscope and How Does it Work?

Chartscope is an iOS-only mobile application designed as an AI-powered tutor for cryptocurrency chart analysis. Unlike traditional trading platforms that bombard users with dashboards and buy/sell buttons, Chartscope focuses purely on education. It answers questions like “Why did this Doji candle form?” or “What does the RSI line crossing 70 mean?” without ever suggesting you trade.

The app operates on a simple principle: learn by asking. You connect your read-only exchange API keys (Binance, Coinbase, or Kraken), and the AI analyzes your charts in real time. Every feature — from the AI Chat to the AI Vision tool — is built to teach, not to signal.

How the AI Works Under the Hood

Chartscope uses a two-layer AI architecture. The first layer runs entirely on your iPhone using Apple’s Core ML framework. According to the site documentation, this on-device trend classification completes in approximately 1 millisecond on the iPhone Neural Engine. The second layer — for conversational AI — uses a cloud-based failover chain to answer complex questions about your charts.

This hybrid approach means core analysis happens locally, while advanced explanations benefit from cloud AI without storing your data. The result? You get instant feedback on trend direction without waiting for server responses.

What You Can Actually Ask the AI

The AI Chat feature is context-aware — it knows which coin you’re viewing and what timeframe you’ve selected. You can ask questions like:

  • “Why is this Shooting Star pattern significant on the 4-hour chart?”
  • “What does the MACD histogram divergence mean here?”
  • “Is there a conflict between RSI and the moving average crossover?”

The AI never gives signals. It always explains the why behind the pattern, which is exactly what a crypto education app should do.

Key Features for Learning Crypto Chart Analysis

Chartscope packs three core features that work together to build your understanding of technical analysis. Each one targets a different learning style — reading, seeing, and analyzing.

AI Chat: Your Personal Crypto Tutor

The AI Chat is the centerpiece of the app. It supports multi-turn conversations, meaning you can ask follow-up questions without losing context. For example, after asking about a Doji candle, you can say “What does that mean for momentum?” and the AI remembers the previous candlestick reference.

AI Vision: See Patterns You Might Miss

The AI Vision feature lets you upload a screenshot of any chart — even from other apps or websites — and get a visual analysis. It identifies candlestick patterns like Doji, Engulfing, and Shooting Star formations. This is particularly useful for beginners who can spot a pattern in a screenshot but don’t know what it’s called or what it implies.

Important: AI Vision requires separate consent. You control when visual AI is active, and you can disable it independently from the text-based AI Chat.

Insights v6: Structured Learning Cards

The Insights feature generates 9 structured cards covering volatility, momentum, risk/reward ratio, and indicator conflicts. Each card includes a Market Mood thermometer (scored 0-100) and quantifies the risk/reward relationship. Instead of guessing whether the market is “overbought,” you get a clear number and explanation.

For example, if RSI shows overbought conditions but the MACD is still bullish, the Insights card will flag that as an indicator conflict and explain why both readings matter. This teaches you that no single indicator tells the whole story.

Privacy and Security: Chartscope’s On-Device Approach

Privacy is not an afterthought in Chartscope — it’s the foundation. The app was built with a “privacy by design” philosophy, meaning no personal data is ever collected or transmitted.

What Data Stays on Your Device

According to the privacy policy, Chartscope does not collect: - Personal identification information - Email addresses or contact details - Location data - Usage analytics or tracking data - Your API keys or credentials - Your portfolio data or trading history

Your exchange API keys are stored exclusively in the iOS Keychain with hardware-level encryption. They never leave your device. User preferences like currency and language are stored locally using iOS UserDefaults. Cached market data may be stored temporarily for performance, but nothing is sent to external servers.

Why On-Device AI Matters for Beginners

Many crypto apps send your chart data to cloud servers for analysis. That means your portfolio positions, entry prices, and trading patterns are visible to third parties. Chartscope’s on-device CoreML processing ensures that zero data is sent for machine learning. The ~1ms inference happens entirely on your iPhone.

For a privacy crypto app, this is a significant differentiator. You can analyze charts without worrying about your data being sold, analyzed, or leaked.

Third-Party Connections (Limited and Read-Only)

Chartscope connects to cryptocurrency exchanges (Binance, Coinbase, Kraken) via read-only API keys. These connections are used solely to fetch market data for display. The app never has permission to trade, withdraw, or modify your accounts. You can revoke API access at any time from the exchange’s settings.

Why Chartscope is Ideal for Crypto Beginners

Step-by-Step: How a Beginner Uses Chartscope

Here’s a practical walkthrough of how a complete beginner would use the app:

  1. Download and install Chartscope from the App Store (iOS 16+ required). Start the 3-day free trial — no credit card needed for the trial period.
  2. Connect a read-only exchange API (Binance, Coinbase, or Kraken). The app walks you through creating an API key with “read-only” permissions. Your key is stored in the iOS Keychain and never leaves your device.
  3. Select a coin and timeframe. Tap on Bitcoin, Ethereum, or any supported asset. Choose a timeframe (15 min, 1 hour, 4 hours, daily, etc.).
  4. Ask the AI a question. Type “What does this candlestick pattern mean?” or “Explain the RSI indicator.” The AI responds in plain language with educational context.
  5. Use AI Vision for screenshots. Take a screenshot of a chart from any source. Upload it to AI Vision. The app identifies patterns and explains their significance.
  6. Review the Insights cards. Swipe through the 9 structured cards to see volatility, momentum, risk/reward, and indicator conflicts. Each card includes a plain-language explanation.
