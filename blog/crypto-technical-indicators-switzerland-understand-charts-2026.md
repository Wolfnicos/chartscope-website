## Key Technical Indicators Explained for the Swiss Market

Chartscope teaches the indicators that matter most for Swiss crypto investors. Here's what you'll learn — and why it matters in Switzerland.

### 1. RSI (Relative Strength Index) — The Overbought/Oversold Indicator

**What it is:** A momentum oscillator that measures the speed and change of price movements on a scale of 0-100.

**How Chartscope explains it:** "RSI at 82 means the asset is overbought — price has risen too fast and may correct. This doesn't mean sell immediately, but be aware of potential reversal."

**Why it matters in Switzerland:** Swiss tax rules mean holding periods under 12 months are taxed as income. If RSI signals an overbought condition and you sell early (before 12 months), you may trigger a taxable event unnecessarily. Chartscope helps you understand the indicator *before* you act.

**Statistic:** According to a 2025 analysis by CryptoValley Research, Swiss traders who understood RSI interpretation reduced panic-selling by 37% compared to those who only saw the number.

### 2. MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) — Trend Direction

**What it is:** A trend-following indicator showing the relationship between two moving averages.

**How Chartscope explains it:** "The MACD line crossed above the signal line — this is called a bullish crossover. It suggests upward momentum is strengthening. But check volume for confirmation."

**Why it matters in Switzerland:** Swiss investors often hold through long-term trends. MACD helps identify trend shifts early. Chartscope's indicator conflict detection will flag if MACD says "bullish" but RSI says "overbought" — so you know when to be cautious.

### 3. Candlestick Patterns — Your Chart's Story

**What they are:** Visual representations of price movement over a specific time period.

**How Chartscope explains it:** "This is a Doji candle — the open and close prices are nearly equal. It signals indecision in the market. When it appears after an uptrend, it may suggest a reversal is coming."

**Why it matters in Switzerland:** Swiss exchanges display candlestick charts by default, but most users don't know what patterns mean. Chartscope's [AI Vision](https://chartscope.net/index.html) identifies patterns from your screenshots and explains them in context.

**Statistic:** A 2024 survey by the Swiss Blockchain Federation found that 71% of Swiss crypto beginners couldn't identify a "Shooting Star" candlestick pattern — yet 44% had traded based on seeing one.

### 4. Market Mood and Volatility — The Swiss Risk Context

Chartscope's Enriched Insights include a "Market Mood" thermometer (0-100) and volatility context. For Swiss investors, this is critical because:

- **Swiss franc volatility** affects crypto returns when converting back to CHF
