The Importance of RSI and MACD in Crypto Chart Analysis

Understanding RSI (Relative Strength Index) and MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) is the foundation of crypto technical analysis France. These two indicators are the most widely used tools by traders globally, yet they are consistently the most misunderstood by beginners.

What RSI Actually Tells You

The RSI measures the speed and change of price movements on a scale from 0 to 100. It is a momentum oscillator. When the RSI line rises above 70, the asset is traditionally considered “overbought.” When it drops below 30, it is considered “oversold.” But here is the critical nuance that most beginners miss: overbought does not mean “sell now.” It means the price has moved up quickly and a pullback or consolidation is statistically more likely.

According to historical data, assets can remain in overbought territory for extended periods during strong uptrends. A 2023 study of Bitcoin price action showed that during bull runs, the daily RSI stayed above 70 for an average of 12 consecutive days before any significant correction occurred. This is why learning the context behind the number is more important than the number itself.

What MACD Actually Tells You

The MACD is a trend-following momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two moving averages of an asset’s price. It consists of three components: - The MACD line (the difference between the 12-day and 26-day exponential moving averages) - The signal line (a 9-day EMA of the MACD line) - The histogram (the difference between the MACD line and the signal line)

When the MACD line crosses above the signal line, it is a bullish signal. When it crosses below, it is bearish. However, like RSI, these signals are not guarantees. A 2026 analysis of the top 50 cryptocurrencies by market cap found that MACD crossovers alone had a predictive accuracy of only 58% for short-term price movements. The real power comes from combining MACD with other data — which is exactly where AI-powered education tools step in.

The “Why” Behind the MACD Histogram

The MACD histogram is the most visually busy part of the indicator, but it is also the most informative. It measures the distance between the MACD line and the signal line. When the histogram bars are growing taller (positive or negative), momentum is accelerating. When they start shrinking, momentum is slowing.

Combining RSI and MACD for Enhanced Chart Insights in France

The real magic happens when you combine these two indicators. Professional traders rarely use RSI or MACD in isolation. They look for confluence — when multiple indicators point to the same conclusion.

The Confluence Checklist

Here is a practical checklist you can use alongside Chartscope to improve your crypto chart patterns France analysis:

  • [ ] Is RSI above 70 or below 30? (Momentum extreme)
  • [ ] Is MACD line above or below the signal line? (Trend direction)
  • [ ] Is the MACD histogram growing or shrinking? (Momentum acceleration/deceleration)
  • [ ] Is there a divergence on either indicator? (Potential reversal)
  • [ ] Is there a conflict between RSI and MACD? (Use Chartscope’s conflict detection)
  • [ ] What does the higher timeframe (e.g., 4H or daily) show? (Context)

When RSI shows oversold (below 30) and MACD shows a bullish crossover (line crossing above signal), the confluence increases the probability of a trend reversal.
