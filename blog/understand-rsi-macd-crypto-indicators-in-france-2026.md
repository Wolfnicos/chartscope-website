Understanding RSI (Relative Strength Index) and MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) is the foundation of **crypto technical analysis France**. These two indicators are the most widely used tools by traders globally, yet they are consistently the most misunderstood by beginners.

### What RSI Actually Tells You

The RSI measures the **speed and change of price movements** on a scale from 0 to 100. It is a momentum oscillator. When the RSI line rises above 70, the asset is traditionally considered "overbought." When it drops below 30, it is considered "oversold." But here is the critical nuance that most beginners miss: **overbought does not mean "sell now."** It means the price has moved up quickly and a pullback or consolidation is statistically more likely.

According to historical data, assets can remain in overbought territory for extended periods during strong uptrends. A 2023 study of Bitcoin price action showed that during bull runs, the daily RSI stayed above 70 for an average of 12 consecutive days before any significant correction occurred. This is why learning the context behind the number is more important than the number itself.

### What MACD Actually Tells You

The MACD is a **trend-following momentum indicator** that shows the relationship between two moving averages of an asset’s price. It consists of three components:
- The MACD line (the difference between the 12-day and 26-day exponential moving averages)
- The signal line (a 9-day EMA of the MACD line)
- The histogram (the difference between the MACD line and the signal line)

When the MACD line crosses **above** the signal line, it is a bullish signal. When it crosses **below**, it is bearish. However, like RSI, these signals are not guarantees. A 2026 analysis of the top 50 cryptocurrencies by market cap found that MACD crossovers alone had a predictive accuracy of only 58% for short-term price movements. The real power comes from combining MACD with other data — which is exactly where AI-powered education tools step in.

| Feature | RSI (Relative Strength Index) | MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) |
|---------|-------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| **What it measures** | Speed and change of price movements | Relationship between two moving averages |
| **Scale** | 0 to 100 | No fixed scale (varies by asset) |
| **Primary signal** | Overbought (>70) / Oversold (<30) | Line crossovers and histogram divergence |
| **Best used for** | Identifying potential reversals | Confirming trend direction and momentum |
| **Common beginner mistake** | Treating overbought as a sell signal | Ignoring market context and volume |
